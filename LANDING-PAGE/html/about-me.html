My Journey Through Life

    Childhood Teenage College

Childhood days

1 My experiences during my childhood mostly happened in Bohol, my province. 2 During this time I was living with my mother while my dad was in Manila. 3 Nothing really happened that much when I was still in kindergarten here in my province. 4 Most days were simple, and everything felt slow and quiet. 5 After six years I was sent here to Manila to continue studying. 6 During my first week here in Manila, when I was still in elementary school, I was really nervous because of the environment. 7 It was new to me, and I didn’t know what to expect. 8 The surroundings felt louder and busier than what I was used to in Bohol. 9 After some time I made new friends, and I was finally confident in my environment. 10 I slowly got used to the city and the people around me. 11 During my elementary days I made a lot of new friends. 12 We played some games both offline and online almost every day. 13 We also talked and laughed at each other a lot. 14 Those small moments made school more fun for me. 15 One of my memorable experiences that I can also remember is when I was in grade 6. 16 In that year, our family mostly went out of town during holidays. 17 We went to different places in northern Luzon, Baguio, Sagada, Banaue, and other places. 18 Each trip felt exciting because I wasn’t used to traveling that far. 19 I remember the long drives, the cold weather, and the beautiful views. 20 Thosse moments became some of the happiest memories of that year.

Teenage years

1 And finally my teenage years being a teenager was really new to me. 2 Everything felt different compared to when I was younger. 3 First I gained a lot of responsibilities, like managing my documents, thinking about what I want to do later in life, and so much more. 4 These responsibilities slowly piled up as I got older. 5 Managing my documents wasn’t my responsibility during my childhood days. 6 Now I manage them every time I send an application. 7 This includes transferring to another school or submitting documents requested by my teacher. 8 Sometimes I even have to check if they are complete or if something is missing. 9 I also have to make sure I submit them on time. 10 Thinking about what I want to do later in life is probably the hardest part of my teenage years. 11 This situation feels uncomfortable because there are too many possibilities. 12 I often do not know which path is right for me. 13 The reason this is difficult is because I was in my comfort zone during those times. 14 I was not used to stepping out of that comfort zone. 15 During this period I was also in high school. 16 My high school years were similar to my elementary days. 17 I got to play with my friends, laugh, and talk with them often. 18 We had fun during breaks and joked around like before. 19 The only real difference was the amount of responsibilities I had to deal with. 20 That is why my teenage years became a mix of comfort and challenge.

College years

1 Lastly, my college days started from my first year and continue up to now which is my second year. 2 A lot of people feel nervous and anxious when they are freshmen in college. 3 I felt curious and anxious because during the pandemic I was homeschooled. 4 When I was homeschooled I did not have classmates or friends to talk to. 5 The only people I could talk to were my relatives and family members. 6 Most of the time I stayed inside the house doing my activities. 7 I was no longer used to interacting with people my age. 8 When college started I did not know how to make friends anymore compared to high school. 9 It felt strange to suddenly be surrounded by many students again. 10 I did not know how to start conversations or approach other people. 11 After a couple of weeks I was thankful that I made new friends. 12 Up to now in my second year they are still my friends. 13 We slowly got to know each other through group activities and small talks. 14 They were easy to get along with which helped me adjust. 15 At present I am enjoying what I am doing in college. 16 I get to learn new things and explore what I want to do after college. 17 I also understand more about the course that I am taking. 18 Sometimes college life becomes stressful but it is still manageable for me. 19 I also get opportunities to go to other places because of school requirements or group work. 20 Overall, my college days are shaping the kind of person I want to become.
