Di Pa Perfect, Pero Gumagana: My Python Journey

Christopher Jayce Pacanza
January 29, 2026

Build more, read less

What if the best way to learn programming is not by reading more notes, but by actually building something and watching it break? As a Computer Science student, na realize ko na I learn more when I create projects, even small and imperfect ones. That is why I started building Python projects. This blog is about how I am starting out, what I am working toward, and why these small projects matter to my future in IT. Instead of waiting until I feel “ready,” I decided to just start. Kahit simple calculator lang or small logic-based programs, each one teaches me something new. I began to understand that learning happens more when I struggle with errors than when I just read about concepts. Sometimes breaking the code is actually part of the process, because errors force me to think deeper. For me, learning programming is not about consuming information, it is about actively creating and experimenting.

Starting with Python Projects

Nagsisimula palang ako, so my Python projects are simple. Some focus on logic, some on basic systems, and some on problem solving. These projects help me understand how ideas turn into working programs. I do not aim for perfect projects. What matters to me is learning how to think, debug, and improve my code. Every small bug that I fix builds my confidence little by little. I am slowly training myself to break down problems before coding them. Minsan matagal bago ko ma solve yung isang problem, but that process teaches me patience. I realized that consistency is more important than complexity. Even simple projects can become powerful learning experiences if I take them seriously and reflect on what I learned. At the end of the day, progress is built through repetition and practice.

I hate designing, so my main goal is to become a backend developer. I enjoy working on the logic and structure behind applications more than the visual side. Python is a good starting point for this path because it is widely used in backend systems. I like the feeling of organizing data, creating functions, and making systems work behind the scenes. Designing layouts and choosing colors is not something that excites me. Mas gusto ko yung part na nag iisip kung paano gagana yung system internally. Backend development feels like solving a big puzzle where every piece must connect properly. That is why I am focusing my energy on strengthening my logic and programming fundamentals. For me, mastering the backend means mastering how systems think, and that challenge motivates me. I believe that if I stay consistent, I can slowly shape myself into the developer I want to become.

Every progress matters

Right now, my focus is simple. Keep building Python projects and keep learning. As a CS student, I know that progress takes time. Starting small is not a weakness, it is the first step toward becoming a backend developer. I do not need to rush the process or compare myself to others. What matters is that I am improving compared to who I was yesterday. Kahit maliit na improvement lang bawat araw, malaking bagay na yun in the long run. I trust that consistent effort will compound over time. Programming is a long journey, and I am just at the beginning. What keeps me going is the mindset that every line of code I write makes me better. Because in the end, every progress matters.
